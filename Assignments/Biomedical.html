Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering (BSc. BioMed. Eng.)

The Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering is a dynamic and rapidly evolving discipline that bridges the gap between engineering, biology, and medicine to address some of the most pressing challenges in human health and healthcare delivery. This course equips students with a powerful blend of technical expertise and biological insight, allowing them to design and innovate life-saving technologies that directly impact patient care and medical practice. From the development of advanced imaging systems like MRI and CT scanners, to the creation of artificial limbs, organ support devices, biosensors, and wearable diagnostics, biomedical engineers are at the forefront of engineering solutions that improve lives. The curriculum provides a robust foundation in core areas such as mathematics, physics, mechanics, electronics, and computer programming, while also diving deep into subjects like human anatomy, physiology, biomaterials, biomechanics, and tissue engineering. Students gain hands-on experience through state-of-the-art laboratory work, simulation-based learning, clinical case studies, and interdisciplinary projects that encourage collaboration with medical professionals and researchers. There is a strong emphasis on innovation, ethical responsibility, and regulatory standards, preparing students not just to build devices, but to understand the environments in which these devices operate — inside the human body or within hospital systems. With opportunities to specialize in areas like medical robotics, neural engineering, rehabilitation technology, or drug delivery systems, graduates of Biomedical Engineering are well-prepared to enter a variety of fields including clinical engineering, medical device development, biomedical research, or even pursue further studies in medicine or biotechnology. This course is ideal for students who are passionate about both technology and human well-being, and who want to apply problem-solving skills in a field that literally saves lives and enhances quality of life around the world.

What You’ll Learn

  • Human Physiology for Engineers – Understanding the body through a technical lens.
  • Biomechanics – Applying mechanical principles to the human body.
  • Biomaterials – Designing and testing materials for implants and devices.
  • Biomedical Instrumentation – Tools and systems for diagnosis and monitoring.
  • Medical Imaging – Techniques like MRI, CT, and ultrasound.
  • Bioinformatics & Data Analysis – Managing biological and health-related data.
  • Rehabilitation Engineering – Designing assistive and prosthetic technologies.
  • Capstone & Clinical Applications – Real-world medical challenges and solutions.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Clinical lab experiences and biomedical device workshops.
  • Internships with hospitals, research labs, and med-tech companies.
  • Capstone projects focused on solving real healthcare challenges.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped to work in a wide range of fields, including:

  • Medical Device Design and Manufacturing
  • Healthcare Technology Management
  • Rehabilitation Engineering
  • Biomedical Research and Development

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Biomedical Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Cental Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Biomedical engineers are at the forefront of healthcare innovation, improving patient outcomes through technology. This course prepares you to design solutions that enhance medical care and quality of life.
