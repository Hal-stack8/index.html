Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering (BSc. Ind. Eng.)

Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering is a dynamic four to five-year undergraduate program that equips students with the knowledge and skills to design, improve, and manage complex systems that integrate people, processes, and technology. It blends engineering principles with business, analytics, and human factors to streamline operations and enhance efficiency across diverse industries. Students learn the foundations of operations research, production planning, ergonomics, systems design, logistics, and supply chain management. Emphasis is placed on quantitative decision-making, optimization techniques, and data-driven approaches to solve real-world industrial problems. The program also includes modules on quality control, engineering economics, project management, and enterprise systems. These prepare students to assess workflow, reduce waste, enhance productivity, and align technological innovation with organizational goals. Industrial engineering thrives at the intersection of technical expertise and managerial insight. Students are exposed to simulation tools, process modeling software, and real-time analytics, often engaging in internships, case studies, and capstone projects to bridge theory and practice. Graduates of Industrial Engineering are highly sought after in sectors such as manufacturing, healthcare, logistics, finance, aviation, and consulting. With a strong foundation in both engineering and operations strategy, they become systems thinkers, process optimizers, and strategic leaders. Whether it’s improving patient flow in hospitals, optimizing delivery networks, or automating production lines, industrial engineers drive transformation in the modern world—and this journey begins here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Operations Research – Data-driven decision making for optimization.
  • Manufacturing Systems – Efficient production processes and technologies.
  • Human Factors Engineering – Designing systems that work well with people.
  • Supply Chain Management – From raw materials to product delivery.
  • Quality Control – Ensuring products and services meet standards.
  • Engineering Economics – Cost analysis and financial decision-making.
  • Systems Simulation – Modeling complex industrial systems.
  • Data Analytics & AI Tools – Leveraging technology for smart manufacturing.

Practical Application

This program blends technical knowledge with real-world experience through:

  • Industrial attachments and internships in manufacturing plants, logistics firms, and consultancies.
  • Simulations and case studies on real supply chain, production, and systems challenges.
  • Project-based learning focused on optimizing workflows, productivity, and cost-efficiency.
  • Collaborative capstone projects with industry partners aimed at solving actual business or production problems.

Career Opportunities

Industrial Engineering graduates are highly versatile, opening doors in sectors such as:

  • Manufacturing and Production Management
  • Logistics, Supply Chain, and Operations Planning
  • Healthcare Systems and Service Industry Optimization
  • Consulting Firms and Business Process Improvement
  • Quality Control and Project Management
  • Tech Startups, Financial Services, and Data Analytics

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Industrial Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Cental Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Industrial Engineering plays a crucial role in making organizations run better—whether that’s improving efficiency on a factory floor, optimizing hospital workflows, or streamlining global supply chains. It blends engineering with business thinking, preparing you to lead innovation, reduce waste, and enhance productivity in nearly every industry.

Ideal For Students Who…

  • Enjoy finding smarter, faster, and more efficient ways to do things.
  • Have a strong interest in both engineering and business problem-solving.
  • Are analytical, detail-oriented, and systems thinkers.
  • Want a flexible career that spans manufacturing, healthcare, logistics, and beyond.
