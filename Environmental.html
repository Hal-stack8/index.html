Bachelor of Science in Environmental Engineering (BSc. Env. Eng.)

Bachelor of Science in Environmental Engineering is a forward-looking four to five-year undergraduate program that prepares students to solve some of the world’s most pressing environmental challenges. By integrating principles from civil engineering, chemistry, biology, and environmental science, this degree equips students with the knowledge and tools needed to protect human health and preserve the environment for future generations. The program covers core areas such as water and wastewater treatment, air quality management, solid and hazardous waste management, environmental impact assessment, environmental chemistry, and sustainable design. Students are trained to engineer systems and technologies that reduce pollution, restore ecosystems, and promote the sustainable use of natural resources. Through a blend of classroom instruction, laboratory experiments, fieldwork, and industry collaboration, students learn how to analyze environmental systems, design solutions, and apply regulatory frameworks. Skills in modeling, geographic information systems (GIS), environmental data analysis, and risk assessment are emphasized throughout the course. BSc. in Environmental Engineering graduates are in high demand across sectors including government agencies, environmental consultancies, non-governmental organizations, water utilities, energy firms, and construction and mining industries. They often work in roles related to environmental compliance, sustainability planning, water resources management, pollution control, and climate resilience. Whether mitigating the effects of climate change, improving public health through clean water access, or designing sustainable cities, environmental engineers are essential in building a greener, more resilient future—and it all begins here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Environmental Chemistry – Understanding chemical processes in air, water, and soil.
  • Water & Wastewater Engineering – Design of treatment plants and water distribution systems.
  • Air Quality Management – Monitoring and controlling atmospheric pollutants.
  • Solid & Hazardous Waste Management – Sustainable strategies for waste disposal and recycling.
  • Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) – Evaluating the ecological consequences of projects.
  • Hydrology & Hydraulics – Managing surface and groundwater systems.
  • Sustainable Design & Green Infrastructure – Building systems with environmental integrity in mind.
  • GIS & Environmental Data Analysis – Mapping and modeling ecosystems with advanced tools.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Fieldwork in water, air, and soil sampling and analysis.
  • Environmental lab experiments and simulation modeling.
  • Internships with environmental agencies and green-tech firms.
  • Capstone projects focused on solving pressing sustainability issues.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are prepared to make meaningful impact across sectors, such as:

  • Water and Wastewater Treatment
  • Environmental Impact Assessment and Policy
  • Renewable Energy and Climate Resilience
  • Consulting, NGOs, and International Development

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Environmental Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Cental Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Environmental engineers are at the forefront of solving the planet’s most urgent challenges—from clean water access and pollution control to climate change adaptation. This course equips you to drive sustainable solutions that protect both people and the planet.

Ideal For Students Who…

  • Enjoy solving complex problems with real-world impact.
  • Have strong interests in science, math, and engineering principles.
  • Desire a career that contributes to public health and ecological balance.
