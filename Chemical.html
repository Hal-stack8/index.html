Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering (BSc. Chem. Eng.)

Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering is a dynamic four to five-year undergraduate program designed to train highly skilled engineers who can transform raw materials into valuable products through sustainable, safe, and efficient processes. As a multidisciplinary field that bridges chemistry, biology, physics, and mathematics with engineering principles, Chemical Engineering is central to addressing global challenges such as energy, food production, clean water, health, and climate change. Students are equipped with core knowledge in chemical reaction engineering, thermodynamics, process control, fluid mechanics, heat and mass transfer, and separation processes. They also gain exposure to the design and operation of chemical plants and industrial systems that convert materials into everything from fuel, plastics, and pharmaceuticals to food additives and clean water. This program emphasizes hands-on learning through lab work, pilot plant training, simulation software, and capstone design projects. Students develop competencies in critical thinking, data analysis, process optimization, safety management, and sustainable design. As part of the program, industry attachments and research opportunities give students a real-world perspective and technical edge. BSc. in Chemical Engineering graduates are well-suited for careers in energy, petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, food processing, environmental management, materials science, and biotechnology. They often work in process design, product development, environmental regulation, operations, and technical consulting. The course also provides an excellent pathway for postgraduate study in chemical, environmental, biomedical, or materials engineering. Whether it’s designing renewable energy systems, developing new materials, creating cleaner manufacturing processes, or ensuring food and drug safety, chemical engineers shape the world at a molecular level—and it all starts here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Thermodynamics – Understanding energy balances in chemical systems.
  • Transport Phenomena – Heat, mass, and momentum transfer in processes.
  • Chemical Reaction Engineering – Designing reactors for efficient chemical transformation.
  • Process Control – Monitoring and controlling industrial chemical processes.
  • Separation Processes – Techniques like distillation, filtration, and extraction.
  • Process Design & Simulation – Modeling full-scale chemical plants and systems.
  • Materials Science – Understanding the properties of industrial and nanomaterials.
  • Special Topics – Petroleum, Food Processing, Biochemical, and Environmental Engineering.

Practical Application

This program integrates theoretical knowledge with real-world chemical engineering experience through:

  • Extensive laboratory experiments in reaction kinetics, fluid dynamics, and process control.
  • Industrial attachments in chemical plants, refineries, or manufacturing facilities.
  • Design projects that tackle real industrial challenges, including sustainability and process optimization.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of Chemical Engineering are highly versatile and can pursue careers in:

  • Petrochemical and Energy Industries
  • Pharmaceuticals and Biotechnology
  • Food Processing and Consumer Goods Manufacturing
  • Environmental Engineering and Waste Management
  • Process Design, Safety, and Industrial Consulting

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Chemical Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Cental Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply
