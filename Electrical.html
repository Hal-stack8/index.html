Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronics Engineering (BSc. EEE)

The Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronics Engineering is a dynamic four to five-year undergraduate program that prepares students to be world-class innovators, engineers, and leaders of the world's most revolutionary sector today. Being the foundation of contemporary civilization, electrical and electronics engineering drives technological innovation in the energy systems, communications, automation, and electronics industries. This course provides students with a broad foundation in core subjects such as circuit theory, electromagnetics, electronics, digital systems, signal processing, electrical machines, control systems, power systems, and embedded systems. Students also gain insight into how to combine theory and practice through lab exposure to simulation and modeling software, microcontroller programming, and circuit design at the lab scale. The programme is structured to give comprehensive knowledge in both low-voltage electronics and high-voltage electrical systems so that the students are well-positioned to design, build, and maintain smart systems, communication networks, and green power infrastructure. With high emphasis on innovation, sustainability, and problem-solving, the curriculum gives impetus to research new fronts such as renewable energy, smart grids, robotics, Internet of Things (IoT), and artificial intelligence-based engineering applications. With laboratory instruction, industry practice, research, and senior design experience, graduates are not only skilled engineers, but also versatile thinkers capable of solving 21st-century engineering challenges. BSc. in Electrical and Electronics Engineering graduates are ready to find employment in power generation and transmission, telecommunication, automation, aerospace, electronics manufacturing, biomedical equipment, and systems engineering. The curriculum also provides a solid foundation for further specialization at the graduate level in fields such as embedded systems, nanotechnology, control engineering, and energy policy. In a world increasingly reliant on connectivity and electrification, electrical and electronics engineers are constructing the future—and theirs starts here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Circuit Theory – Analyzing electric circuits and their behavior.
  • Electronics – Design and application of diodes, transistors, and amplifiers.
  • Digital Systems – Logic circuits, microcontrollers, and digital communication.
  • Control Systems – Feedback systems, automation, and robotics.
  • Power Systems – Generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity.
  • Electromagnetics – Field theory and wave propagation.
  • Signal Processing – Analyzing and manipulating signals and data.
  • Embedded Systems & IoT –Smart devices and real-time systems.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Hands-on laboratory work and electrical workshops.
  • Industry internships during later years.
  • Capstone projects that solve real engineering problems.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped to work in a wide range of fields, including:

  • Power Generation and Renewable Energy
  • Telecommunications and Control Systems
  • Consumer Electronics and Product Design
  • Automation, Robotics, and Embedded Systems
  • Consulting, Research, and Academia

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Electrical and Electronics Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B− (minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya (EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Electrical and electronics engineers power the modern world—literally and figuratively. This course prepares you to shape how we live, connect, and advance as a society through electrical and digital innovation.

With a focus on sustainability, efficiency, and cutting-edge technology, you will be at the forefront of creating solutions that address global challenges such as energy scarcity, climate change, and the need for smarter infrastructure.
