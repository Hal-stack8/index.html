Bachelor of Science in Mechatronics Engineering (BSc. Mechatronics Eng.)

The Bachelor of Science in Mechatronics Engineering is a forward-looking four to five-year undergraduate program that merges mechanical, electrical, electronics, computer, and control engineering into one integrated discipline—designed for students eager to build the intelligent systems and smart machines of tomorrow. Mechatronics lies at the core of automation and robotics, enabling the creation of innovative solutions like self-driving vehicles, robotic arms, drones, intelligent manufacturing systems, and smart consumer devices. Students are introduced to foundational subjects such as mechanics, electronics, control theory, microcontrollers, sensors, and embedded systems, while developing programming skills and proficiency in system integration. The course emphasizes both theory and practice, offering hands-on experience with tools like PLCs, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, CAD/CAM software, MATLAB, and robotics kits. Through practical labs, design projects, and internships, students are trained to conceptualize, design, and build smart electro-mechanical systems that can sense, decide, and act. Mechatronics engineers are multi-skilled problem solvers who thrive at the intersection of disciplines. Graduates are equipped for careers in industrial automation, robotics, automotive systems, medical technology, aerospace, and smart infrastructure. They can work as automation engineers, robotics specialists, systems designers, or pursue further studies and research in cutting-edge technologies like AI integration, bionics, and IoT systems. With a focus on innovation, adaptability, and real-world impact, this degree positions graduates at the forefront of the technological revolution—where machines become intelligent, and engineering meets the future.

What You’ll Learn

  • Embedded Systems – Microcontrollers and real-time computing.
  • Control Engineering – Feedback systems, automation, and stability analysis.
  • Mechanical Design – Structures, mechanisms, and actuators.
  • Digital & Analog Electronics – Circuit design and signal processing.
  • Robotics – Kinematics, sensors, actuators, and intelligent systems.
  • Programming for Engineers – C/C++, Python, MATLAB for hardware integration.
  • Sensors & Instrumentation – Data acquisition and interfacing with machines.
  • System Integration – Merging hardware and software into smart systems.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Hands-on laboratory work with robotics kits and automation systems.
  • Industry internships in automation, robotics, and smart systems.
  • Capstone projects that design and build integrated mechatronic systems.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped to work in a wide range of fields, including:

  • Industrial Automation and Robotics
  • Automotive Systems and Smart Vehicles
  • Medical Devices and Healthcare Technology
  • Aerospace and Defense Systems
  • Consumer Electronics and IoT Devices

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Mechatronics Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Mechatronics engineers are at the forefront of the automation and robotics revolution, creating intelligent systems that enhance productivity, safety, and quality of life.
