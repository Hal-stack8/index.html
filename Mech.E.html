Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering (BSc. Mech. Eng.)

Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering is a broad four to five-year undergraduate degree program designed to develop tomorrow's engineers, problem solvers, and innovators. As one of the world's oldest and most diverse fields of engineering, mechanical engineering lies at the heart of modern technology and industry. It provides students with a solid foundation in foundation fields of thermodynamics, mechanics, materials science, fluid dynamics, machine design, and manufacturing processes. Through a solid blend of theory and application, students are taught to design, analyze, and maintain mechanical systems that power industries ranging from automobiles and airplanes to energy, healthcare, and robots. Students are taught to apply advanced engineering technology like computer-aided design (CAD), simulation, and automation systems and exposed to emerging technologies in areas of sustainable energy, mechatronics, and additive manufacturing (3D printing). The course program centers on problem-solving, critical thinking, teamwork, and communication—skills that are essential to a career in engineering. The students also get industry attachments, laboratory work, capstone design projects, and research work, which ensure that they graduate with not just knowledge, but also practical skills. BSc. in Mechanical Engineering graduates are well poised to pursue a broad spectrum of career opportunities in product development, mechanical design, systems engineering, manufacturing, consulting, and project management. The course also provides a good platform for postgraduate studies in engineering, innovation, or applied sciences. Wherever they initiate change, from driving technological advancement to constructing infrastructure and creating sustainable engineering solutions, mechanical engineers are leading the way into the future—and it all begins here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Thermodynamics – Energy transfer and transformation.
  • Fluid Mechanics – The behavior of liquids and gases.
  • Machine Design – Creating and analyzing mechanical components.
  • Mechanics of Materials – Stress, strain, and material performance.
  • Dynamics & Control Systems – Predicting and controlling motion.
  • Manufacturing Processes – Modern production methods and automation.
  • CAD & FEA – Simulation and design using software tools.
  • Special Topics – Automotive, HVAC, and Renewable Energy.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Hands-on laboratory work and mechanical workshops.
  • Industry internships during later years.
  • Capstone projects that solve real engineering problems.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped to work in a wide range of fields, including:

  • Manufacturing and Aerospace
  • Automotive and Product Design
  • HVAC, Energy, and Robotics
  • Consulting, Research, and Academia

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Mechanical Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Cental Placement Service(KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya(EBK)
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply

Why It Matters

Mechanical engineers make the physical world work—from cars to power plants to prosthetic limbs. This course prepares you to lead in innovation and engineering impact.

Ideal For Students Who…

  • Enjoy solving technical challenges.
  • Have strong interests in Math and Physics.
  • Are curious about machines and how they work.
  • Desire a versatile and globally respected engineering career.
