Bachelor of Science in Aeronautical Engineering (BSc. Aeronautical Eng.)

Bachelor of Science in Aeronautical Engineering is an intensive four to five-year undergraduate degree program that prepares students for the dynamic and challenging world of aviation and aerospace systems. Rooted in physics, mathematics, and engineering design, the program empowers learners to understand, analyze, and innovate within the field of flight—both within Earth’s atmosphere and beyond. As one of the most technically demanding branches of engineering, aeronautical engineering blends classical disciplines such as aerodynamics, propulsion, thermodynamics, and structural mechanics with modern topics like flight control systems, avionics, and aerospace materials. From the design and optimization of aircraft to the study of supersonic flow and high-altitude performance, students are equipped with the critical tools and knowledge necessary for working with complex, high-performance flight systems. Throughout the program, students engage with advanced engineering software for Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Finite Element Analysis (FEA), and Computer-Aided Design (CAD), giving them the ability to simulate and evaluate aircraft behavior in a virtual environment. This is complemented by hands-on training in wind tunnels, propulsion labs, and avionics workshops to reinforce theoretical concepts through practical experimentation. The curriculum emphasizes not just technical mastery, but also systems thinking, problem-solving, innovation, and teamwork, with students taking part in real-world projects such as designing UAVs, aircraft subsystems, and even prototypes for sustainable aviation technologies. Additionally, industry internships, capstone design projects, and research opportunities ensure that graduates leave the program with both academic depth and real-world experience. Graduates of Aeronautical Engineering are prepared to pursue careers in aircraft and spacecraft design, aviation systems engineering, aerospace research and development, maintenance and operations, and defense and national security sectors. They are well-suited for roles in aviation companies, space agencies, regulatory bodies, and innovative start-ups tackling the next frontier of flight. Whether it's creating quieter engines, more efficient aircraft, or advanced navigation systems, aeronautical engineers play a central role in redefining how humanity moves through the skies—and it all begins here.

What You’ll Learn

  • Aerodynamics – How air flows over aircraft surfaces and affects flight.
  • Flight Mechanics – Stability, control, and performance of aircraft in motion.
  • Propulsion Systems – Jet engines, turbines, and propulsion technologies.
  • Aircraft Structures – Stress analysis, lightweight materials, and structural design.
  • Avionics & Control Systems – Aircraft instrumentation and automated control.
  • Thermodynamics – Energy systems critical for propulsion and aerospace systems.
  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) – Simulating airflow and optimizing designs.
  • Space Systems – Fundamentals of rocketry, satellites, and orbital mechanics.

Practical Application

This program emphasizes real-world learning through:

  • Wind tunnel experiments and aircraft performance testing.
  • Flight simulators and avionics system labs.
  • Internships with aviation firms, airports, and aerospace agencies.
  • Capstone design projects such as unmanned aerial vehicles or propulsion systems.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are equipped to work in a wide range of high-tech and fast-paced sectors, including:

  • Aircraft and Spacecraft Design
  • Airline Operations and Maintenance
  • Defense and Aerospace Manufacturing
  • Unmanned Aerial Systems and Drone Technology
  • Flight Testing, Simulation, and Avionics
  • Research and Development in Aerodynamics and Propulsion
  • Regulatory Agencies and Aviation Safety Bodies

Minimum Entry Requirements

To be eligible for admission into the BSc. Aeronautical Engineering program, candidates must meet the following minimum requirements:

  • KCSE Mean Grade of B (Plain) or equivalent.
  • C+ in Mathematics and Physics.
  • C in English or Kiswahili.
  • An aggregate grade score of B-(minus) based on the four cluster subjects as recognised by the Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) OR any exception provided by the Engineers Board of Kenya (EBK).
  • Relevant diploma holders as recognised by the departmental senate may apply.
